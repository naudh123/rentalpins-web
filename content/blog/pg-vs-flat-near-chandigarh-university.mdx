Choosing between a **PG and a flat near Chandigarh University** is one of the first housing decisions CU students make — and the wrong choice often shows up only after you have paid session deposit.

This guide compares real trade-offs renters face in Kharar and adjoining belts, without promising one option always wins.

## When PG makes sense near CU

Managed PG near Chandigarh University works well when you want:

- **Meals included** — fixed monthly food cost without daily cooking
- **Lower upfront friction** — furnished room, basic Wi‑Fi, and laundry often bundled
- **Structured rules** — curfew and security that parents prefer for first-year students
- **Walkable corridors** — Kharar town and Kharar–Landran road clusters sit close to CU gates

Most student PG rooms in the CU belt range from roughly **₹3,500 to ₹8,000 per month** depending on sharing, AC, and meal plan — always confirm extras with the owner.

## When a shared flat wins

A **flat near Chandigarh University** suits students who:

- Cook together or prefer independent meals
- Want fewer house rules than hostel-style PG
- Have a stable group of two to four roommates with agreed rent split
- Need more space for study, guests, or internships gear

Popular flat belts include **Kharar town**, **Sunny Enclave**, and **Gillco Valley**. Total rent is higher, but per-person cost can beat premium PG once you split a 2–3 BHK.

## Cost comparison checklist

Before deciding PG vs flat, build a simple monthly table:

1. **Headline rent**
2. **Food** — PG meals vs groceries and cooking gas
3. **Electricity and AC** — metered or included?
4. **Wi‑Fi and laundry**
5. **Commute** — auto, bike, or walk time to CU
6. **Deposit and notice period**

The cheapest headline PG is not always cheapest once food and AC surcharges are added.
