“Should I rent or buy?” is really three questions: how long you will stay, how much flexibility you need, and how much due diligence energy you have right now. This guide stays practical — no promised returns, no fake price forecasts.

## When renting usually wins

Renting tends to fit:

- **Students and first-year relocators** — especially near [Chandigarh University](/blog/kharar-pg-guide-chandigarh-university) and Mohali colleges  
- **Job transfers under 2–3 years** — flexibility beats locking capital  
- **Households still choosing sectors** — rent in two areas before you commit

Use the [rent map](/search) to filter PG, flats, or houses by locality. Owner-direct contact helps you compare real monthly cost including food, utilities, and commute.

## When buying may be worth serious research

Buying enters the conversation when:

- You expect a **long stay** (often 5+ years — your math may differ)  
- You have **document bandwidth** — title review, society transfer, loan prep  
- You found a **micro-market you already know** from renting or working nearby

Start on [RentalPins Buy](/buy/search) and read the [Mohali investment guide](/buy/mohali-investment-guide) plus [New Chandigarh guide](/buy/new-chandigarh-investment-guide) for corridor context — not as predictions, but as navigation.

## Compare monthly cost honestly

Renting: rent + maintenance + commute + broker-free search on RentalPins.

Buying: EMI or capital outlay + maintenance + property tax + transaction costs + renovation buffer.

Headline rent ₹25,000 vs EMI ₹35,000 is not the whole story either way. Build a simple table for *your* candidate flat.
